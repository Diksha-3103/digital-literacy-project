## 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist
1. Secure Your UPI Transactions: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Scammers often send "Collect Requests" on apps like GPay or PhonePe, claiming you’ve won a scholarship or prize. A PIN is only for sending money.

2. Verify Before You Scan: When using QR codes at local stalls or student fests, double-check that the name appearing on your screen matches the vendor. Fraudsters sometimes paste their own QR stickers over legitimate ones.

3. Use "Locked" Profiles & Private Handles: On platforms like Facebook or Instagram, use the "Profile Lock" feature (popular in India) to prevent strangers from downloading your photos or taking screenshots for "catfishing" or deepfake misuse.

4. Avoid Campus 'Free' Wi-Fi for Banking: Open library or cafe Wi-Fi networks are often unencrypted. Use your mobile data or a trusted VPN when logging into your student portal, email, or banking apps.

5. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Go beyond passwords. Use an authenticator app or SMS codes for your Gmail and WhatsApp accounts to prevent "SIM swapping" attacks.

6. Check for 'HTTPS' & Padlocks: Before entering your roll number or credit card details on any site (especially for exam fees), ensure the URL starts with https:// and shows a small padlock icon in the browser bar.

7. Identify 'Job Offer' Scams: Be wary of Telegram or WhatsApp messages offering "Part-time work from home" that ask for a small "processing fee" or "security deposit" first. Real companies never ask for money to hire you.

8. Audit App Permissions: Regularly check which apps have access to your contacts, microphone, and gallery. Many "free" study or photo-editing apps are actually data-miners in disguise.

## How to Report a Cybercrime in India
If you or a friend fall victim to a digital scam, every minute counts—especially for financial fraud where "Golden Hour" reporting can help freeze the stolen funds.
Official Website: cybercrime.gov.in (The National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal)
National Helpline: Call 1930 immediately to report financial fraud.
Local Action: You can also visit the nearest Cyber Cell at your local police station.
