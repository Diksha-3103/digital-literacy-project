# Case Study: The Ghost in the Machine
## The Crime:
Identity theft occurs when someone steals personal information—such as a Social Security number (SSN), credit card details, or login credentials—to commit fraud. A prominent real-world example is the 2017 Equifax Data Breach, which exposed the private data of nearly 147 million people, providing a "gold mine" for identity thieves for years to follow (Source: Federal Trade Commission, 2017).

## How It Typically Happens:
Data Harvesting: The criminal gains your info via phishing emails, buying "leaked" data on the dark web, or "skimming" your card at a gas pump.
Verification: They "test" the info by making a tiny, $1 purchase to see if the account is active.
Account Takeover/Creation: The thief changes your billing address to their own so you don't receive alerts. They then apply for high-limit credit cards or personal loans in your name.
The "Cash Out": They max out the credit lines and disappear before the first bill is ever generated.

## Who is Targeted?
While anyone is a target, thieves often hunt for "clean" SSNs. This makes college students and children primary targets because they rarely check their credit scores, allowing the crime to go undetected for years.

## The Consequences:
The aftermath is devastating. Victims often face rejected loan applications, frozen bank accounts, and years of legal battles to prove they didn't spend the money. It can take hundreds of hours of paperwork to "clean" a credit report, potentially delaying milestones like buying a car or renting an apartment.
